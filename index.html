skype heartbreak show

COMING APR 2015 | FACEBOOK GROUP

+ - - - - - - - - - - - +

Skype Heartbreak Show is an interactive installation exploring the decay of a long-distance, romantic relationship through the filters of unreliable technology.

During the performance, the audience will witness the development of the couple's relationship, as mediated by the technologies though which the characters communicate. The storyline is dynamic, changing according to the movement of the audience within the performance space. Members of the audience are both spectators and actors in this digital love story.

The performance will explore the nature of glitch. Technology, like us, have the potential to act in spastic, violent, unpredictable ways. In glitch, data decays. Over enough time, the repositories of our modern lives (e-mails, instant messages, shared pictures and videos) inevitably decay into a state of legacy and incoherence. The information becomes unreliable, even for the machines that originally transmitted and cached them. Digital memory becomes no more reliable than human memory.

Team Heartbreak

Pierre Depaz is a second-year graduate student at NYU Tisch Game Center. After completing a Masters in Political Studies in France, he has worked for world-class game developers in Paris, emerging contemporary artists in Shanghai and creative agencies in NYC. A previous recipient of the GSO Interdisciplinary Grant, he has spent his summer performing Treason, the resulting project, while also participating in a residency at Concordia University in Montreal, always exploring the relations of power emerging from our more and more natural use of technology.

Ansh Patel is a first-year MFA candidate at NYU Tisch Game Center. Before graduating from Mumbai University with computer engineering major, he was an experimental game developer with most of his works exploring the boundaries between the player and developer, while bringing societal issues on intersectionalism to the fore. His game on existentialism and systemic oppression, Exist was a chosen selection at a number of festivals across Asia and North America, where it won “Most Innovative Game” honors. He has also worked as a game and music critic with his work appearing in web publications like Unwinnable, Paste Magazine and Arcade Review. He has also been a film critic for Next Projection and covered international film festivals like New York Film Festival, Inside Out and NIFF for them.

From a very young age Tommy Payne was encouraged to explore life through artistic means. Born in Perth, Western Australia she has traversed the globe with a bold enthusiasm to enact change through better design. She has taught art to children living in Nepalese slums, contributed design work for social change incubator, YGAP, and has returned to school as a first-year MPS candidate in the Interactive Telecommunications Program, NYU Tisch. She completed a Bachelor of Behavioral Science at the University of Notre Dame in Perth before moving to Melbourne where she graduated as the top student in her class at the School of Design, RMIT. She has spent the past few years working for global advertising agency M&C Saatchi as an art director where she enjoyed creating branded content for digital, print and multimedia. Collaborating, creating interactive works, and diversifying her artistic identity are her primary focus whilst she is in NYC.

Pat Shiu is currently a first-year graduate student at NYU’s Interactive Telecommunications Program. Previously, Pat worked in Beijing and Singapore, leading the Asia Pacific design team at advertising agency Godfrey Q as Senior User Experience Designer. Her clients include J.D Power, Symantec and Norton Antivirus. She also comes from a background of innovation consulting, and has conducted workshops on creative methodologies and creative process management for clients such as Standard Chartered and SHELL. In her work, Pat is interested in exploring the use of nostalgic technology, particularly mass consumer electronics, as storytelling tools.
